Our Heritage

A Tradition Passed Down
Through Generations

Pickling is one of India's oldest food preservation traditions, dating back thousands of years. In every Indian household, grandmothers and mothers would spend summer days carefully preparing batches of pickles — filling large clay pots with raw mangoes, lemons, and spices — a labor of love that would last the whole year.

At Home Made Pickles & Snacks, we carry that tradition forward. Our recipes come straight from handwritten notebooks passed down through three generations. We use the same sun-drying techniques, cold-pressed sesame oil, and whole spice blends that our grandmothers swore by.

The Art of Pickling in India

Indian pickles (called achaar) are a cornerstone of every meal. The process varies by region — in Andhra Pradesh, pickles are fiery and oil-heavy; in Gujarat, they're sweet and mellow; in Karnataka, they're tangy and aromatic.

Traditional pickling relies on salt, oil, and spices to create an environment where food stays preserved naturally for months — sometimes years — without refrigeration. The result is a complex, layered flavor that only deepens with time.

Our snacks follow the same philosophy — made from scratch using stone-ground flours, freshly cracked spices, and traditional cooking methods that can't be replicated by machines.
